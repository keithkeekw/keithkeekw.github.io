Book Review: Make Time

2020-11-04
book
book review

Authors: Jake Knapp & John Zeratsky
Type: Non-Fiction
Genre: Productivity
Links: Goodread.com | Amazon.com

Summary

All of us are have 24 hours a day to live our life and most of us will complain about insufficient time to actually live in the way we wanted and to pursue activity or experience we truly enjoy. Make Time offers an easy framework to carve out some time of our day to do things or tasks that matter the most to us and the framework only consists of 4 elements.

Make Time is a framework for choosing what you want to focus on, building the energy to do it, and breaking the default cycle so that you can start being more intentional about the way you live your life. Even if you don’t completely control your own schedule—and few of us do—you absolutely can control your attention.

The diagram and the explanation depict what is this book all about:

*Excerpt from the book:

1. Highlight: Start each day by choosing a priority
Asking yourself “What’s going to be the highlight of my day?” ensures that you spend time on what matters most to you and don’t lose the entire day reacting to other people’s priorities.

2. Laser: Beat distraction to make time
Distractions like email, social media, and breaking news are everywhere. We’ll show you how to adjust your devices and apps so you can find Laser mode.

3. Energize: Use the body to recharge the brain
The lifestyle defaults of the 21st Century ignore our evolutionary history and rob us of energy, but with a few small changes, you can reset those defaults and power up.

4. Reflect: Adjust and improve your system
Finally, before going to bed, you’ll take a few notes. It’s super simple: You’ll decide which tactics you want to continue and which ones you want to refine or drop.

The authors share lots of ways and methods for each elements which you can try to implement it. Apart from what they have listed in it, you can modified or use an entire new methods to incorporated into the framework.

This framework is not tailor-made for any specific individual but it is works like a foundation to a building-block to your Make Time process.

Key Insights

1. There’s always time in our busy lifestyle.

Regardless how busy are you in your life, there’s always time for yourself to do things that you care or enjoy the most like learning new programming language, reading books or going out for a walk with your family.

Busy Bandwagon 1 is a vicious cycle and we should not make this part of our mindset.

2. Build your own Make Time process by experimenting it.

With the framework from this book, there are lots of method which we can use to “make more time” in our life. Method such as:

  • Remove all the Infinite Pools 2 application from your smartphone.
  • Design your day by blocking a specific time of the day to do carry out your work/tasks/anything that matter to you in full capacity of concentration.

All the methods mentioned in the book might not be applicable to you but you can always give a try by experimenting it for a week or two. If it fails, at least, you get the taste of the method and you can share it with your family and friends.

3. The flexibility of the framework.

Both authors encourage to mix and match various methods in the framework based on the scenario or situation at the given time.

Conclusion

You only waste time if you’re not intentional about how you spend it.

From this book, I learned that most of the time I have been throwing my time away without any knowledge about it. Most of time on watching silly videos from YouTube and constant refreshing the Twitter stream from my iPhone and iPad.

Changing defaults isn’t always easy, so it’s helpful to look back on the day through a grateful lens.

Even though I have not fully implement this framework in my life, I am starting to be more intentional with my time. Step-by-step, I am changing some some part my time management like waking up early to do all the mundane chores and work on things which I enjoy the most just like writing this post.

I like this framework because it is easy to remember and flexible to implement it.

Ratings

⭐: 5/5
